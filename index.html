Did You Know?

Fascinating facts about Bangladeshi flora

Bangladesh has over 5,000 plant species, of which hundreds are found nowhere else on Earth.

The Sundarbans — world's largest mangrove — hosts the famous Sundari tree (Heritiera fomes), giving the forest its name.

The White Water Lily (Shapla) is Bangladesh's national flower, blooming across rivers and ponds every monsoon season.

The Jackfruit (Kanthal) is Bangladesh's national fruit — a single fruit can weigh up to 55 kg!

Bangladesh is one of the world's top rice producers, cultivating over 7,000 rice varieties adapted to different flood levels.

Over 500 medicinal plants are used in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine practices across Bangladesh's villages.

Neem (Nim) is called "village pharmacy" — nearly every part of the tree has medicinal value in Bangladeshi folk medicine.

The bamboo forests of the Chittagong Hill Tracts are among the most diverse in South Asia, with 31+ bamboo species.
